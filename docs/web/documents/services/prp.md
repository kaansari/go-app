# PRP Q&A



| **FAQ**                                 | **Answer**                                                                                                                              |
|----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **What is Cannula PRP Treatment?**           | Cannula PRP treatment involves using a blunt-tipped cannula to inject Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) into targeted areas for skin rejuvenation.|
| **What is Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)?**      | PRP is derived from the patient's blood, containing concentrated platelets, growth factors, and healing components beneficial for skin.|
| **How does Cannula PRP differ from Needles?**| A cannula, with its blunt tip, minimizes tissue trauma compared to needles, reducing bruising and ensuring a smoother PRP distribution.|
| **What areas can be treated with Cannula PRP?**| Cannula PRP is versatile, suitable for facial rejuvenation, under-eye hollows, nasolabial folds, jawline definition, and hand rejuvenation.|
| **Is Cannula PRP painful?**                   | Discomfort is minimal due to the blunt-tip cannula and topical anesthetic application, ensuring a comfortable treatment experience.                   |
| **How long does a Cannula PRP session take?** | Sessions typically last 60-90 minutes, including preparation and treatment, offering a convenient lunchtime procedure for busy individuals.|
| **How many sessions of Cannula are recommended?**        | Optimal results often require 3-4 treatments, spaced 4-6 weeks apart, ensuring comprehensive rejuvenation and long-lasting effects.             |
| **When will I see results of Cannula?**                  | Initial improvements may be visible within weeks, with continued enhancement over months as the skin regenerates and revitalizes.|
| **How long do the results last?**             | Results are enduring, often persisting for 12-18 months, with maintenance sessions available to prolong and enhance outcomes further.|
| **Are there any side effects or downtime?**   | Side effects are minimal, with potential for slight swelling or bruising, allowing for a swift return to daily activities post-treatment.|
| **Is Cannula PRP suitable for everyone?**     | Generally safe for most individuals, a consultation with a qualified practitioner is essential to assess suitability and expectations.|
| **Can it be combined with other treatments?** | Yes, Cannula PRP can complement other aesthetic procedures like dermal fillers or laser therapy, offering a holistic rejuvenation approach.|
| **Is there any post-treatment care needed?** | Adhering to aftercare instructions, such as sun protection and skincare, optimizes and prolongs the results of Cannula PRP treatment.|
